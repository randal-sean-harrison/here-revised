Health and Safety Practices

Practice Physical Distancing

General Rules

  • Stay at least 6 feet (about 2 arms-length) from others
  • Avoid crowded spaces or spaces where physical distancing guidelines cannot be observed
  • We encourage the use of stairs inside campus buildings whenever possible, walking up and down stairs on the right-hand side.
  • Most elevators should be limited to two occupants per cab. Floor decals will be placed to show where occupants should stand. Those waiting for elevators in lobbies should practice appropriate physical distancing.
  • When outdoors, please walk on the right side of sidewalks and provide at least six feet of distance from pedestrians walking in the opposite direction.

High Traffic/Density Campus Spaces

  • In fixed seat classrooms, students will use only every third seat; in non-fixed seat classrooms, students will be seated in a way that provides each student with 30 square feet of space. Classrooms and other instructional spaces will be arranged to ensure that instructors are a minimum of 6 feet from students
  • Students will have assigned seats to help with contact tracing efforts when necessary
  • We ask faculty members to stagger the dismissal of classes – asking certain rows to exit, and then the next set of rows, etc. – to ensure students leave classrooms in an orderly manner and maintain safe physical distances while exiting. 20 minutes will be provided between classes beginning in the fall
  • There will be opportunities for in-person gatherings provided they meet University and health guidelines, but many co-curricular and extra-curricular activities (lectures, performances, club activities, events) will be held virtually.
  • Dining hall times and lay-outs have been adapted to preserve appropriate physical distancing. In addition, on-campus dining will provide expanded take-out and other options to protect health and safety.
  • Meetings and study sessions should be conducted by phone or online when possible
